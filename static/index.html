🇮🇶 Sounds Arabic Speakers Often Mix Up

P / B Arabic has no "p" sound, so it often comes out as "b" — "pen" sounds like "Ben". Keep your lips closed a beat longer and let a puff of air out for P.
V / W "Vine" and "wine" can blend together. For V, your top teeth touch your bottom lip; for W, round your lips like blowing out a candle.
TH (θ/ð) Often swapped for S/Z/T/D — "think" becomes "sink" or "tink". Put your tongue tip between your teeth and blow air out gently.
Consonant clusters Arabic tends to add a short vowel before clusters — "street" becomes "istreet". Practice starting right on the "s", with no vowel before it.
Short vs. long vowels "Ship" and "sheep" can sound the same. Exaggerate the difference at first — short and sharp for one, longer and tenser for the other.
Stress & rhythm Arabic gives syllables fairly even weight; English leans hard on stressed ones and swallows the rest. That's exactly what "Stress & Rhythm" below trains.
